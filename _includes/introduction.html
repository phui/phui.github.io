About Me


I am a Ph.D. student in the Informatics program at Indiana University Luddy School of Informatics, Computing, and Engineering. My adviser is Dr. Filippo Menczer , working as part of the Observatory on Social Media (OSoMe). My research focuses on the detection and characterization of malicious campaigns on social media. I work a lot on Twitter dataset to

  • detect coordinated user accounts in malicious campaigns
  • characterize malicious account behaviors at scale
  • develop tools that everyone can use to combat malicious campaigns

In particular, I am one of the main developers for BEV and BotSlayer (see my projects below). I lead the backend developments, from streamer and database to algorithms and containerization.
